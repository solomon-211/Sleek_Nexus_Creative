Become a Mentor

Guide the next generation of South Sudanese tech professionals with your experience and insight.

What Mentors Do

One Conversation Can Change a Career

Many young professionals and students in South Sudan are talented but lack access to experienced people who can guide their decisions — which skills to learn, how to build a portfolio, how to navigate the job market, how to start a tech business.

As a mentor, you commit to regular 1-on-1 sessions (typically 1–2 hours per month) with one or more mentees. You share your experience, review their work, and help them avoid mistakes you've already made.

  • Regular 1-on-1 mentoring sessions
  • Career guidance and portfolio reviews
  • Flexible schedule — you set your availability
  • In-person or remote sessions
  • Support from the SNC mentorship coordinator

Benefits for Mentors

Mentorship is a two-way relationship. Here's what you get in return.

Give Back

Contribute directly to South Sudan's technological development by guiding talented young people in the field.

Expand Your Network

Connect with other mentors, the SNC team, partner organizations, and the wider tech community in South Sudan.

Recognition Certificate

Receive a formal Sleek Nexus Creative Mentor Certificate at the end of each mentorship cycle.

Stay Sharp

Explaining your knowledge and helping others problem-solve keeps your own skills and thinking fresh.

Who Can Become a Mentor?

You don't need to be famous or have a prestigious title. You need practical experience and the desire to help.

Software Developers

Any level of experience in web, mobile, backend, or full-stack development.

Designers

UI/UX designers, graphic designers, and product designers with portfolio experience.

Tech Entrepreneurs

Founders, startup operators, and business owners with tech experience and hard-won lessons.

Educators

Teachers, trainers, and curriculum developers with experience in tech education.

Diaspora Professionals

South Sudanese professionals abroad who want to give back remotely through mentorship.

Data & Analytics Pros

Data analysts, data scientists, and business intelligence specialists.
